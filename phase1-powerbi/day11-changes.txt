1. Simplified the color scheme and improved contrast
   I replaced the bright yellow background with a clean, dark blue theme and consistent color palette. This reduces visual noise and improves readability, allowing users to focus on the
   data rather than being distracted by harsh or inconsistent colors.


2. Replaced cluttered visuals with clearer chart types
   I removed overcrowded visuals (like the dense pie chart and overly detailed line chart) and used simpler, more appropriate visuals such as bar charts, line charts, and KPI cards. This 
   makes comparisons easier and helps users quickly understand key trends and insights.


3. Improved layout and visual hierarchy
   I organized the dashboard into structured sections with proper spacing, aligned elements, and clear headings. Key metrics (Total Sales, Total Orders) are placed at the top for quick 
   visibility. This logical flow enhances user experience by guiding attention to the most important information first.
